9. Unit Cost Distribution by Item (Pricing Consistency & Outlier Detection)

Shows the spread of unit costs for each item. Range bars show min–max, dots show individual orders. Brush a price range to highlight orders by Region.

Chart 1 — Revenue Concentration: The donut chart reveals that just three reps — Jardine, Parent, and Jones — account for over 50% of total sales revenue, indicating high dependency on a small group of performers. This concentration poses a risk if any top performer leaves or underperforms.

Chart 2 — Seasonal Patterns: The streamgraph shows significant revenue spikes in Q4 of both years, driven primarily by Binder orders, suggesting end-of-year procurement cycles. Pencil sales remain steady throughout, acting as a baseline revenue stream, while Desk sales are rare but high-value events.

Chart 3 — Order Size Distribution: The trellis histogram reveals that Pencil orders are spread across a wide range of units (7 to 95), while Desk orders cluster at very low quantities (2–5 units). The mean overlay shows Binder orders average around 45 units, the most consistent order size across all items.

Chart 4 — Regional Revenue Over Time: The stacked area chart shows that Central region dominates monthly revenue in most months, but East region drives occasional high spikes (e.g., Parent's $1,619 Binder order in Jul 2021). West region contributes the least and most inconsistently, with long dormant stretches.

Chart 5 — Price vs Volume Trade-off: The bubble chart reveals a clear pattern: most high-unit orders occur at low unit costs (Pencils at $1–5), while high unit-cost items (Desks at $125–275) are sold in very small quantities. The shape encoding confirms that West region reps rarely appear in the high-volume, low-cost quadrant, suggesting they focus on niche, high-value sales.

Chart 6 — Regional Item Mix: The stacked bar shows Central region generates the most revenue overall, but its revenue is heavily skewed towards Binders and Pen Sets. East region has a more balanced item mix. West region’s revenue is dominated by a single large Binder order and Desk sales, making it the least diversified.

Chart 7 — Rep Performance Tiers: The brush-filtered ranking reveals a clear three-tier structure: Jardine and Parent are top performers (over $2,800 each), a middle tier of 5–6 reps clusters around $1,000–2,000, and Andrews and Howard lag significantly below $1,000. The detail breakdown shows top reps derive revenue from multiple item categories, while lower performers tend to rely on a single item.

Chart 8 — Activity Gaps: The heatmap highlights that several reps have multi-quarter gaps in sales activity. When filtered to the West region, only Sorvino and Thompson are active, and neither has sales in every quarter — creating geographic coverage risk. Central region, by contrast, has consistent activity from at least 3–4 reps every quarter.

Chart 9 — Pricing Inconsistency: The strip plot exposes that identical items are sold at widely varying unit costs — Pen Sets range from $4.99 to $23.95, and Binders from $1.99 to $19.99. Brushing reveals these variations occur across different reps and regions, suggesting inconsistent discount practices or pricing authority that warrants standardisation.
